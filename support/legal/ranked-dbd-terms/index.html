Preamble & Information

Welcome to the Ranked DBD Terms for Ranked DBD by the DBD Comp Network. The Ranked DBD is a free-to-play service by the DBD Comp Network and is bound to the DBDCompNet Account.

It is to understand following:

“Ranked DBD is not competitive but it is also not default Dead by Daylight. It offers Dead by Daylight under certain rules and obligations. It is not directed at the default playstyle or the standard playstyle in competitive settings. You will find differences that you may not know from playing normal competitive games or from playing the game as it is intended without any rules. These rules change the way Dead by Daylight is played ‘normally’ and works on its own system and matchmaking rules.”

The Ranked DBD Rules may also be referred to as the “Ranked DBD Terms of Use”.

Ranked DBD is not official by Behaviour Interactive or Dead by Daylight. Support is exclusive via support@dbdcompnet.com or via Discord.

Chapter I. | General Information regarding Ranked DBD

Basic Game Rules

1. Killer Blocking, Camping & Securing

a.) When there is no other obvious way out, a killer may not block a survivor’s path for more than 8 seconds. The timer does not affect the killer when there is at least one way to avoid the blocking killer.

b.) A killer may not block the hook in a way that two survivors, each from a side, can not perform an unhook or see the unhook prompt.

c.) Any other non-explained (banned) type of killer body blocking is allowed.

d.) The timer for each of the rule articles starts when the killer moves into a blocking position. The timer does not stop when the survivor tries to mind-game the killer in close proximity to the blocking door / pathway. The timer ends when the killer leaves close proximity to the blocking pathway or door.

Clarification

“Blocking” another players’ - in this case the killer blocking the survivors - pathway is considered when the player is not able to pass by the killer or through the killer and the survivor is actively trying to achieve just that.

Penalty - When found guilty of breaking this rule, the killer is penalized with a set forfeit.

2. Opening Chests

a.) Survivors are not allowed to finish opening default chests.

b.) Standard-Penalty applies for breaking this rule.

3. Self-Unhooking

a.) Survivors are not allowed to attempt a Self-Unhook action if they do not have the perk “Deliverance” or “Wicked” equipped.

Clarification

Penalty - If a survivor attempts to Self-Unhook without having the perk “Deliverance” or “Wicked” - even when not accomplishing a self-unhook - the set is forfeit by the current playing survivor team.

Penalty 2 - If a survivor self-unhooks themselves and that self-unhook does impact the game significantly, the survivor player is suspended.

4. Sabotaging Hooks

a.) Survivors are allowed to sabotage hooks with a toolbox, but not using perks.

Clarification

Penalty - If the sabotage action - using a perk - has impacted the game (or could have potentially impacted the game), the survivor team automatically forfeits the set.

5. Abandoning

a.) Abandoning the on-going game is an admission that your team has lost the set and a decision to move on, in the correct set order, to save time, nerves and to keep the games interesting.

b.) Your team may abandon in the middle of a game when it is clear that your team has already lost the winning condition for the set. This does result in 5.a.

c.) Your team may not abandon in the middle of a game if you have already achieved the winning condition for that set. If you do abandon, you do agree to the consequences in 5.a.

d.) The killer may not abandon, unless all survivors are incapacitated and don’t have any second-chance perks. This includes sets that allow at least one second-chance perk, even if no survivor is in possession of a second-chance perk.

e.) Killers may abandon when 5.d does not apply or when 5.d applies but at least one of the opposing survivors has abandoned.

f.) Players may ‘skip’ a set when all members of the team that has yet to play the set, agree on it. Skipping is subject to 5.a and results in its consequences.

g.) Players may be forced to abandon in accordance with 5.a when a rule’s penalty is to enforce a 5.g abandon action.

6. Errors, Bugs & Competitive Advantage

a.) Using errors or bugs in game design is not allowed. It does not matter whether you gain a competitive advantage or not.

b.) Players have an obligation to communicate any type or error or bug within game design with the opposing and within their own team.

c.) Outfits and other cosmetic articles that bring a competitive advantage are not allowed.

Clarification

Banned - known - cosmetics and outfits are: currently no known information.

Penalty - Violating this rule results in the violating team having to reset and not being able to change their perk loadout. The opponent may change the loadouts.

Balancing Information

7. Basic Information

a.) Balancing that players use within the structure of Ranked DBD can be decided by the participating players. A balancing can only be chosen when the majority agrees upon one. When no balancing can be found, the default balancing will be played.

b.) Any balancing is allowed. We advise you to learn about balancing before playing one that you are not familiar with.

We are not represented on Balanced by Daylight.

Clarification

Credits - We recommend using our balancing - default balancing - as it fits perfectly into our rule structure.

Penalty - Violating the chosen balancing will result in a disqualification and a reset of the match. The violating team may not change their loadouts - except for the violating item, perk, add-on or offering being removed BUT not replaced - however, the opposing team may change their loadouts.

8. Scoring & Winning Condition

a.) The Ranked DBD scoring works as follows: (i) Number of generators completed by the survivors, (ii) number of hook stages that were achieved by the killer & (iii) number of fresh hook stages that were achieved by the killer.

b.) Method 8.a.i is the priority method and affects the result and winning condition. When both teams meet the same 8.a.i quota, 8.a.ii will take over. When both teams meet the same 8.a.i and 8.a.ii quota, then 8.a.iii takes over.

c.) In the case that both teams achieve the exact same result in each category mentioned in 8.a, the entire set is replayed from the beginning until there is a clear winner.

d.) To achieve the win you must at least reach the winning condition.

e.) The winning condition is always to overperform the previous team’s result by at least (i) one generator, (ii) one hook stage or (iii) one fresh hook stage.

f.) Escaping a match by taking the hatch (secondary escape) before all 5 generators have been completed is considered a kill of that escaped survivor. Does not apply when all 5 generators have been completed.

g.) If the survivor escapes by taking the exit gate (priority escape) before all 5 generators have been completed, the survivor is considered an escaped survivor. Also applies when all 5 generators have been completed.

h.) A survivor that dies by intentional game design (e.g. bleeding out, reverse bear trap, memento mori / death by hand, end-game collapse, cage of atonement) is considered a kill and awards all hook stages, including a fresh hook stage, for the killer within the scoring.

i.) Scoring is fixed. No other scoring method may or can be used.

Clarification

Example (i) Winning Condition - When the previous team playing survivor has achieved the result 2 generators completed and 4 survivors died, the winning condition for the opposing team is to complete at least 3 generators with 4 or less survivors dead.

Example (ii) Winning Condition - When the previous team playing survivor allowed the killer to get 7 hook stages, the winning condition for the opposing team is to not allow the killer more than 7 hook stages.

Example (iii) Winning Condition - When the previous team playing survivor allowed the killer to get 3 fresh hook stages, the winning condition for the opposing team is to not allow the killer to get more than 3 fresh hook stages.

A “fresh hook stage” is when a survivor has been hooked for the first time in a match.

Chapter II. | Registration Agreement

Playing Requirements

9. Basic Information

a.) All requirements must apply before registration, unless they are marked as ‘non-mandatory’.

10. Requirement A: Language

a.) You must speak and be fluent in English. Our server and this service is primarily made for English users. This rule is mandatory.

b.) You may speak a different language when you are in a pre-made lobby or all players agree upon a different language. This rule does not apply once a member of staff joins. Staff members playing the service may also not agree to speak a different language.

11. Requirement B: Game Knowledge

a.) Players are required to have obtained a split amount of knowledge about the game and how it works. In numbers you must have a minimum amount of 800 hours before playing Ranked DBD. This rule is non-mandatory.

Clarification

This rule protects players' sanity. Some may find Ranked DBD too difficult or too competitive, there we advise you to play the game normally before playing Ranked DBD.

12. Requirement C: Communication & Maturity

a.) Players are asked to understand the competitive callouts (clock callouts) but are not required to. We indicate that not knowing these callouts may result in people ‘dodging’ a player, which is allowed.

b.) We do not have an age requirement for playing Ranked DBD as Dead by Daylight on its own has one. We require a certain level of maturity and professionalism within this structure. This rule is mandatory.

13. Requirement D: Standard Protocols

a.) All players are required to follow our standard protocols. Protocols can be found in chapter 3.

b.) All protocols are mandatory.

Basic Ranked DBD Information

14. Queue Information

When queuing for a ranked match, a player’s MMR (Match-Making-Rating) - known also as SBMM (Skill-Based-Match-Making) by Behaviour Interactive - is taken into consideration. When the system does not find a player in your class region, it will take the next highest (lower to you) player that is sat in the queue.

The system prioritizes the longest in the queue player. When the longest in the queue player is picked, the system looks for the next player in the same class who has waited the longest. If there is no player is the same class, the system will continue with the next lower class and the player who has waited the longest in that class.

15. Play with Friends

a.) To play with friends, all players in the party must have a DBDCompNet Account.

b.) Players may play in a party of maximum 8 and at least 2 individuals. A team persists of 4 players, including 4 survivor players and one of them also plays killer.

c.) Parties of 5-8 do not count towards your MMR points and will not leave a record. They are so-called custom games.

16. Match-Making-Rating Information

When playing Ranked DBD players can receive and lose MMR points (Match-Making-Rating Points). These points are used and necessary to match you with players that meet your level, but also to queue you together with others as fast as possible. The MMR is calculated by certain fixed settings and will be added or deducted from your current score. All players start with no MMR points upon registration.

Chapter III. | Ranked DBD Protocols

Safety & Security Protocols

17. Anti-Cheat Protocols

a.) By registering and playing Ranked DBD you agree to our Anti-Cheat protocol which ensures a fair and secure environment. Our Ranked DBD staff is authorized - under our privacy policy - to conduct searches of your PC / Computer’s virtual information. Searches are conducted in the form of a screenshare.

b.) Failing to follow these searches will result in an immediate permanent suspension of your Ranked DBD services.

c.) Any type of unlocker is forbidden. Audio-Enhancers are forbidden. Stretch resolution is forbidden.

d.) Only windows 10 and 11 are allowed. No other OS that differs from those will be tolerated.

e.) Any form of bypassing before or during our searches is not allowed. Taking over 10 seconds to start a screenshare is considered a hiding attempt. Deleting files, clearing logs, registries and more is considered a hiding attempt.

f.) Filters that provide a competitive advantage are not allowed.

g.) We have automatic filters in place. Before queueing into a lobby you are thoroughly checked for any unusual protocols, files or other on your PC. This information is not saved and is only being checked. If you decide to leave Dead by Daylight after being sent into the queue, the queue will be ended for you until you undergo the same checks again.

Clarification

A competitive advantage using a filter can be that the red stain is more visible than without on certain objects or maps. Perhaps when using a filter the red stain is more visible through walls.

Penalty - Using third-party services that are marked as contraband, will lead to a permanent ban for Ranked DBD.

18. Fair play Protocols

This protocol’s goal is to maintain a fair play and fun environment for all individuals. It regulates classic Dead by Daylight on a new level.

a.) Match-Making is automatically filtered by the region you chose to play in. Upon registration you may choose your region. This decision cannot be changed later. By faking your region - which can be checked via background systems and in-game ping - you are subject to Anti-Cheat and found guilty of using third-party services.

b.) The player may not have a higher ping than 100 (consistent and spiking) when playing killer on their own home host. If the player does have a higher ping than 100, then they are not allowed to play killer.

c.) Players may not queue up with players that have a significantly lower MMR than the player with the highest MMR.

Chapter IV. | Ranked DBD Playing Guidelines

Match Setup

19. Balancing, Picks & Bans

a.) The balancing of Ranked DBD can vary. The users in a lobby may agree upon one balancing. The picks & bans for that matchup can either be done via dbdcompnet.com/easybal/p-b or killers can be agreed upon.

20. Regional Restrictions

a.) The system connects players from the same regions or servers. Players connect to the closest servers of their current location.

b.) Players may choose, at their own risk, to be matched with anyone. The system then ignores the closest region and matches players with anyone.

c.) VPNs (Virtual Private Network(s)) are not allowed.

d.) Players with a home host ping of at least 80 are not allowed to play killer. However, if both teams agree upon the player being able to, this rule is void.

21. Voice Chat Requirement

a.) Players of a lobby must be in a voice channel on Discord together.
