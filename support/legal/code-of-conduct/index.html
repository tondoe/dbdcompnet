I. — Introduction & Scope

The DBD Comp Network Code of Conduct (further “Code”) is the basis agreement for organizations—including other definitions and personal declarations like League, Company, Community—within the DBDCompNet structure.

This Code is for our own and others’ safety, security and mainly trust between the organization (further “Client” or “Applicant”) and the users—including us—of the platform. It regulates how the Client is expected to act and display themselves, as they represent the DBD Comp Network with their actions.

The Code, in its full documentation, is valid for all current and future Clients of our web-based service. This agreement (Code) supplements our general Terms of Service for Clients.

II. — Appearance & Values

The appearance of Clients is legal, organized and friendly towards any outside standing persona or other Client. They organize a legal and friendly competition between competitors, without any favouritism or neglect of others. They are in compliance with Behaviour Interactive’s Terms of Use (“BHVR Terms”) when using their material. The Client is original and not copied from another Client. Their public appearance has very limited controversy and no major incidents left unresolved or ignored.

Major incidents can be ignored by this Code when a plausible and reasonable explanation for certain actions, that struck such an incident, were made. This lies at the sole discretion of the reviewing member of staff or decision made by the administration.

The Client is not publicly against our own values—Black Lives Matter, LGBTQIA+, Feminism—and does not restrict others from expressing them. Expression can be denied once it includes sensitive political topics and will be ignored from this Code.

III. — (Client) Organization Definition

An organization is when a group of minimum two (2) natural persons create a community within the Discord structure—we work with Discord servers only—and are represented publicly on at least one (1) social media platform—excluding Discord—without a minimum of active members.

We work with new and old organizations to create a safe and secure environment for all competitive players.

IV. — Extern Representation

By using our platform, Clients are representing the DBD Comp Network to all external parties—e.g. BHVR—that perhaps don’t know what we are about. This representation is taken seriously.

Information I. — Organizations with official recognition

When an organization is officially recognized by Behaviour Interactive (further “BHVR”) or Dead by Daylight (further “DBD”) it is not guaranteed that such parties are accepted into the DBD Comp Network platform. We will check organizations for our Code regardless of their affiliation with BHVR or DBD and will deny any that violate such said Code.

Information II. — Unable to check your Organization

If the DBD Comp Network (further “We”, “Us”, “Our” or “DBDCN”) is unable to perform their checks on an Applicant the application is rejected without any further explanation or comment towards the Applicant.

The Applicant will be blocked for ninety (90) days from applying again.

Information III. — Ending the Client Relationship

Information regarding how and when the relationship between you and us can be ended is explained in the general Terms of Service section V. — Termination & Suspensions number 2.

Information IV. — Custom Agreement

The DBDCN and the Client can come to custom terms—Agreement—when the Client wishes so. The DBDCN may decline such requests and can also reject any further applications in the future. Such custom agreements include an indemnification clause that states “we are not liable for any fees or damages done to the law in such custom agreements and that the Client who proposed such agreement will be financially liable for all expenses caused by this agreement”.
