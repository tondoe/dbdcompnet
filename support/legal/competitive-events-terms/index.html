These Competitive Events Terms apply to DBD Comp Network events and tournament structures. Event-specific rules may add further instructions where staff publishes them.

Definitions

Core event terms include match, game, set, team, player, main roster, sub roster, staff, suspension, penalty, and competitive advantage.

Preamble

The competitive rule set is copyrighted by DBDCompNet and is used for official events, tournaments, and related competitive structures.

Section 1 - Basic Rules

DBDCompNet tournaments may be one-time or recurring events, may include charity components, and require player registration through the website.

Section 2 - LAN Terms & Conditions

LAN events may apply regional, age, ID, venue safety, media recording, guest conduct, and accommodation rules.

Section 3 - Playership Agreement

Players must follow tournament rules, cooperate with fair-play and security checks, maintain account access, and remain respectful, sportsmanlike, and compliant with DBDCompNet and Discord rules.

Section 4 - Additional Ruling

Event-specific rules published by staff or in official event spaces can apply alongside the main event terms.

Section 5 - Regional Restrictions & VPN

Regional host, ping, and VPN restrictions may apply to preserve match fairness and protect tournament integrity.

Section 6 - Apps and Client Modifications

Unauthorized apps, file modifications, or tools that provide informational or gameplay advantage are prohibited unless staff explicitly permits them.

Section 7 - Fairness Agreement

DBDCompNet reserves the right to enforce fair judgment, reject unfair rules, and prevent unreasonable competitive advantages.

Section 8 - Pre-Match and Lobby Setup

Teams must follow picks and bans instructions, respect deadlines, and set up lobbies and loadouts within official timing rules.

Section 9 - Scoring and Winning

Scoring is based on generators, hooks, deaths, and escapes, with tie handling, set structure, and forfeit handling defined by the event rule system.

Section 10 - Forfeits, Unplayable Conditions, Disconnects

Teams may forfeit under defined conditions, and technical issues must be reported immediately so staff can decide on restarts or penalties.

Section 11 - Survivor Gameplay Rules

Unique survivor requirements, chest restrictions, self-unhook rules, gate timing, and similar gameplay restrictions may be enforced.

Section 12 - Killer Gameplay Rules

Bodyblocking and hatch timing restrictions apply where staff considers them necessary for fairness.

Section 13 - Voice Chat and Streaming

Outer communication restrictions, voice-channel rules, break timing, and stream delay requirements may apply during live competition.

Section 14 - Loadouts and Restrictions

Characters, perks, items, add-ons, offerings, and balancing restrictions must follow the published event rules and submission deadlines.

Section 15 - Team Roster and Appearance

Main roster and sub roster sizes, player identity, and roster lock rules may be enforced per tournament format.

Section 16 - Prizes and Payouts

Prize eligibility, deductions, payment windows, disqualification effects, and the TDSD-01 form may apply before any payout is processed.

Section 17 - Rule Enforcements

Warnings, standard penalties, suspension penalties, restarts, and forced forfeits may be used by staff depending on the severity and impact of a violation.

Section 18 - Confidentiality and Final Say

Staff decisions are final inside the event structure, and sensitive player information must be handled confidentially.
