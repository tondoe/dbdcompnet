Privacy Policy

Welcome to the DBD Comp Union privacy policy (“Policy”). This policy exists for transparency and explains how we process your information and work to protect your privacy. Responsibility for this policy and data collection can be found in the imprint.

1. Scope and Services

1.1 Scope of Policy

This applies when you (“User”) use our Services (defined below) offered by the DBD Comp Union or any of its affiliates or partners (“we”, “our”), unless an affiliate has published their own privacy policy.

The terms of this policy together with our Terms of Service apply to your use of the Services. In certain circumstances, we may provide additional privacy-related information. This policy supplements such other notices.

1.2 Definition of Services

This policy gives you information on how we collect, use, process and disclose Personal Data that you provide us through the usage of our websites, applications, your DBDCompNet Account, social media channels, user support and other services provided by the DBD Comp Union (the “Services”).

2. Data Collection

2.1 Type of Personal Data

We may collect, use, store and share different kinds of personal data about you, including:

  • Contact Data: email address.
  • Content Data: information stored on your device, including login information.
  • Employment Data: information provided for staff applications.
  • Identity Data: legal names, usernames or similar identifiers.
  • Marketing/Communication Data: your preferences for marketing and communication.
  • Profile Data: username, password, registered organizations, support requests.
  • Technical Data: IP address, general location, browser/device info, OS/platform.
  • Usage Data: preferences and statistics about how you use our Services.

We may also collect Aggregated Data (statistical or demographic). Aggregated Data is not Personal Data because it does not directly or indirectly reveal your identity.

We do not collect special category data (race, religion, political beliefs, sexual orientation, health data) unless you provide express consent.

2.2 Failing to provide Personal Data

When we are required to collect Personal Data by law or contract and you fail to provide it, we may not be able to provide Services or features. In that case, we may cancel Services or restrict access.

3. Our Use of Your Data

3.1 Purposes

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): to operate your account, provide Services, support, and communication.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR): to comply with law, investigations, and protect safety and rights.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR): where required for certain content, features, or marketing preferences.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR): troubleshooting, fraud prevention, bans/suspensions, updates, testing, and security.

3.2 Opting out

You can ask us to stop sending marketing messages at any time. Opting out does not affect Personal Data processed for providing Services.

3.3 Changes in purpose

We use Personal Data only for the purposes stated in this policy. If we need to use it for another purpose, we will notify you and explain the legal basis, where required by law.

4. How Personal Data Is Collected

4.1 Direct interactions

  • Registering for Services or creating an account
  • Entering contests, events (tournaments), surveys, or creating forum content
  • Contacting support, requesting help, or providing feedback
  • Applying for positions at the DBD Comp Union

4.2 Data available publicly

If you post Personal Data in public areas (e.g., forums), it may be read or used by others. You disclose such data at your own risk.

4.3 Cookies and automated technologies

We use cookies and similar tools (e.g., log files, analytics/metrics tools) to operate, improve, measure usage, and secure our Services. Where required, non-essential cookies are used only with consent and can be controlled via your browser or cookie preferences.

4.4 Third parties

We may receive Personal Data from service providers, public sources, business partners (“Union Members”), advertising partners, or other third parties where you have agreed they may share data with us.

5. Disclosure of Personal Data

5.1 DBD Comp Union members

Members of the DBD Comp Union may have access to Personal Data where necessary to provide Services, in compliance with this Policy.

5.2 External third parties

We do not share Personal Data with external third parties unless:

  • Fraud and legal: data is requested for legal reasons or to prevent harm/scams.
  • Law enforcement: authorities request data for law enforcement purposes.

If someone requests your Personal Data without a valid reason under this policy, we will generally deny such requests and may contact you first where appropriate.

6. Location of Personal Data

Personal Data may be hosted and processed on servers operated by trusted partners (e.g., in Europe such as IONOS SE). Transfers outside the EEA/UK/Canada/US may occur and we take steps to ensure appropriate safeguards.

7. Duration of Personal Data

We keep Personal Data only as long as necessary to provide Services, maintain your account, and satisfy legal requirements. We may anonymize data for statistical purposes.

8. Safeguarding Personal Data

We use procedures and security features to protect Personal Data. If a Personal Data breach occurs, we will inform you and regulators where required by law.

9. Your Rights

  • Request access, correction, or deletion of your Personal Data
  • Object to processing or request restrictions
  • Request transfer (data portability)
  • Withdraw consent at any time

10. About Children

Our Services are not intended for children/minors under local law. We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children without guardian consent.

11. Third-Party Links

Our Services may include links to third-party sites. We do not control those sites and are not responsible for their privacy practices.

12. Direct Marketing

We send marketing communications only where permitted by law and/or with your consent. You can withdraw consent or unsubscribe at any time.

13. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy. Updates will be posted and may require acceptance to continue using the Services.

14. Contact

Questions or requests: support@dbdcompnet.com
