DBD Comp Network Event Terms

DBD Comp Network Competitive Rules

The DBDCompNet Competitive Rules are created for specific events by the DBDCompNet Management that include competitive (Esports) games, like tournaments.

Download TDSD-01 PDF
Definitions regarding basic terms, competitive terms, tournament terms & rules

Match | A match in terms of our rulebook and usage is one singular in-game match played / being played.

Game | A game in terms of our rulebook and usage is defined as all full sets have been played or are being played.

Set | A set in terms of our rulebook and usage is two killers of the same kind have been played or are being played by two different teams or more. The plural “sets” refers to more than one set.

Match Official | A match official in terms of our rulebook and usage is a person or several people that regulate matches and games. They check in the background if everything is going according to the rules.

Suspension | A suspension in terms of our rulebook and usage is a denial of a player or team to participate in events. The plural “suspensions” refers to more than one player or team or to a singular player stating they have more than one suspension.

Player | A player in terms of our rulebook and usage is an individual participating in a competitive event and is registered on the DBD Comp Network.

Team | A team in terms of our rulebook and usage is a group of 4 or more players.

Main Roster | The main roster in terms of our rulebook and usage are the actively participating players of a team within a tournament structure.

For Example: Team X has 8 players. 4 players of that team are participating in the Y tournament and are on the main roster.

Sub Roster | The sub roster in terms of our rulebook and usage are the backup group of 3 players that can jump in whenever a main roster player falls back.

Staff | The staff in terms of our rulebook and usage is an official member of our DBDCompNet Team.

Official Warning | An official warning in terms of our rulebook and usage is a warning after a rule offence has been committed. May also be referred to as Red Card.

Competitive Advantage | A competitive advantage in terms of our rulebook and usage is the influence or change of the standard and essential conditions of playing Dead by Daylight.

Penalty | A penalty in terms of our rulebook and usage is a consequence for breaking a rule.

Rules | The rules in terms of our rulebook and usage are the official standard in tournaments hosted by us.

Preamble

The DBD Comp Network Competitive Rules are copyrighted under national and international law and may not be copied or used by third-parties.

These rules are governed and used by the DBDCompNet Staff Team for competitive events held within its structure.

§1 Basic rules

DBDCompNet tournaments are not a seasonal event and possibly do not return.

Tournaments done by the DBDCompNet can include charity. Charity is chosen from different public organizations and donations will be fully sent there.

Tournaments of the DBDCompNet may only be joined by registered players of the dbdcompnet.com website. No exceptions are made. We do not deny any applicant that has the right, using the web-service, to apply for a tournament or join it.

Our tournaments include world wide regions.

We may update and/or change these rules at any time.

§2 LAN terms & conditions

The DBDCompNet may host personal LAN (Local Area Network) tournaments within its local region (Germany & Europe). External regions, outside of the European Union or Europe, can be held when all conditions for an arrival are met.

The DBDCompNet LAN may only be entered at the age of 18 or older. Parental supervision does not grant access to our events when below 18 years of age. You will need to provide accurate ID information. For verification we allow your local government ID or your passport.

During LAN events we, the DBDCompNet Staff Team, may take pictures and videos of the entire event that may include you or others. By buying a ticket or appearing at the event, you agree that we are allowed to take pictures of you.

At the DBDCompNet LAN we expect all individuals to behave accordingly. We may assist you out of the event with no chance of a refund.

For guests backstage and with access to players directly, we forbid any kind of direct contact initiated by the viewership. Contact that is initiated by the players is entirely on their own choice.

For safety and security purposes we forbid clothing that hides faces, hands and too thick clothing within the venue. Weapons, drugs and other objects or substances that can cause harm and pain are not allowed.

Unless stated otherwise, the DBD Comp Network is responsible for paying the players’ accommodations like hotels, flights, drives and other.

§3 Playership agreement

The Player agrees to abide by these rules and understands that he may be punished under these and additional rules, within the structure of tournaments.

For safety, fairness & security purposes we may conduct searches of a players’ computer files, more specifically any files, to determine whether a player has violated rules or not. A player may object to the search but, in accordance with these rules, will be found guilty of accused violation.

Players that have no access, or lost access, to their DBDCompNet Account (the “Account”) are automatically disqualified from the tournament.

Players understand the structure of competition and friendly-competition. No hate or ‘bad blood’ will influence their behaviour within the structure of the tournament.

Players agree to join the DBD Comp Network Discord Server (disc.dbdcompnet.com) and remain a member of it during the entirety of the tournament. They also agree to abide by the Discord Server Rules and Discord’s Terms of Service and Community Guidelines.

The DBDCompNet Tournament Code of Conduct is as follows:

The players must uphold standards in fairplay, sportsmanship and respect towards any individual of our playerbase: inside and outside of the tournament (further ”competition”). In case of a code of conduct violation, players may face one of three possible punishments: (1) team disqualification, (2) individual suspensions, (3) red cards / official warnings.

Sportsmanship is defined as the respect for the rules, balancing and enforcement. It also implies that teams or individuals respect other players’ effort and achievements. Players may and will only discuss results or techniques as they happen or after they have happened on-stream (DBDCompNet Twitch) to avoid major or minor spoilers.

Fairplay is defined as playing the game fairly without gaining any kind of competitive advantage due to spoilers, cheats or any other kind of unfairness.

Respect is defined as respecting and working for a friendly and welcoming environment between all individuals. Intentional disconnects - to avoid playing the game normally or allowing an on-stream defeat to happen are considered disrespect towards the players and the tournament organizing end - will be punished via a disqualification, or further a suspension.

Any form of violation of the Tournament Code of Conduct will lead to a Standard Penalty I.

§4 Additional ruling

All tournaments may contain additional rules and guidelines which are not part of these rules. Additional rules do also apply as long as not nullified by §7.

Rules that are posted within event specific channels (for example via Discord) count as if they were written down within these rules as long as they are not nullified by §7.

If an additional rule would violate these rules, the additional rule takes priority before these rules unless nullified by these rules §7.

Depending on the additional rules of a tournament format can the team size vary apart from the team size set by the DBD Comp Network Website.

§5 Regional restrictions, VPN

Tournaments undergo these special rules to generate start-up matches. Following ongoing match flows: these rules set additional requirements and restrictions for matches not under our control (continuous participation / bracket system).

If the killer player from a team is from:

Europe or Russia (EU)

At least two survivors playing for that set for the same team must be from Europe or Russia (EU)

North America East (NAE)

At least 2 survivors playing in that set for the same team must be from North America East (NAE)

North America West (NAW)

At least 2 survivors playing in that set for the same team must be from North America West (NAW) and in total, all four players must be from NA

South America (SA)

At least 3 survivors playing in that set for the same team must be from South America (SA)

Asia, including India and excluding the Middle East (ME)

All four survivors playing in that set for the same team must be from Asia, including India, or Middle East (ME) or Oceania (OCE)

Oceania (OCE)

All four survivors playing in that set for the same team must be from Oceania (OCE)

Middle East (ME)

All four survivors must be either from the Middle East (ME) or Asia, including India

Players with an average ping of 85 and above - including spikes - on their home host if their region has an official Dead by Daylight game server, are due to fairness, not allowed to compete as killers.

Regions with Dead by Daylight game servers are:

Europe (Russia connects to Europe and Asia)

North America

Asia (Russia connects to Asia and Europe)

Australia

South America

Killers in regions that do not have servers at all or connect too far - decided at the staff’s own discretion - away from their home region are not allowed to play killer.

When found guilty of breaking this rule or attempting to break this rule, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty I (including a Standard Penalty IV. when the violation has been found as a mandatory effect of the results).

VPNs (Virtual Private Network) to change your region and to worsen your ping are not allowed. VPNs to avoid higher ping and improve your connection are allowed.

When using a VPN service to improve your ping, you may be allowed to play killer if you are part of the §5.3 sanctioned group of players, at the discretion of our staff team.

Breaking this rule or attempting to break this rule will give the individual player a Suspension Penalty II.

The ping of an individual player may be tested by the staff and match officials ahead of a tournament..

§6 Apps, client modifications

Within the structure of competition events we do not allow modifications that change the natural game look and/or design apart from filters.

When breaking this rule or attempting to break this rule will give the violating team a Standard Penalty IV.

Within the structure of competition events we do not allow applications (“Apps”) that allow you to get insight on the opponents view: For example to see their perk duration.

Breaking this rule or attempting to break this rule will give the violating team a Standard Penalty IV. and Standard Penalty II. when the violation proves to have had a mandatory effect on the match played or Standard Penalty II. when the effect of the apps can not be proved as mandatory for the result.

Modifications or Apps that can calculate or get insight on the opponents positions or any game mechanic is not allowed.

Breaking this rule or attempting to break this rule will give the violating team a Suspension Penalty II. (including a Standard Penalty V.) when the modification or app can be proved as having had a mandatory effect on the match’s result or a Suspension Penalty I. (including a Standard Penalty IV.) when the modification or app can not be proved as having a mandatory effect on the result.

All other Apps or client modifications are not allowed but may be used when the staff decides they’re allowed.

Breaking this rule, and using an app or modification that is either banned by default in accordance with §6 number 4 or the staff has denied, or attempting to use such modification or app will give the violating player a Suspension Penalty II.

Dead by Daylight files may only contain exact original files and may not suffer from any outside changes or overrides. Using Dead by Daylight requires the acceptance of the Easy-Anti-Cheat Terms and the Behaviour Interactive Terms of Use including the Dead by Daylight End-User-License-Agreement.

§7 Fairness agreement, nullifying clause

The DBDCompNet Team agrees to fair judgement within our tournament structure, that is declared as follows:

The way our exclusive events (Invite-Only) Teams are chosen is based on three factors which are (a) how old a team is, (b) what achievements a team has made in the scene and (c) what that team can do in a high-level event.

Judgement (“Scoring”) within our events is always the same for every single team and tournament.

We follow fair treatment and treat all players and teams equally despite their players or past events.

Rules that nullify fair judgement / treatment are not allowed.

Rules in which players gain a competitive advantage for no plausible reason are not allowed.

§8 Pre-Match setup, lobby setup

In a pre-match setup (also referred to as picks and bans) one player of each opposing team is allowed to join. Within the picks and bans a member of staff is verifying the result. Only after verification of the result is the chosen set valid.

Picks and bans must be completed by the teams when instructed so by our responsible staff. If the team does not compromise another date or does not follow up with the set deadline, the team will automatically agree upon our random sets and be forbidden to use more than 3 perks on all of their members.

When one or more teams have not met the deadline of our picks and bans instruction under any means the violating team / teams will receive a Standard Penalty II.

When one or more teams have not worked with our staff to compromise an individual deadline or fail to follow said individual deadline, the team / teams are punished with a Standard Penalty III.

The lobby for a match is set up 10 minutes in advance. It is created by the killer player, unless another agreement has been made or the administration has ordered a different host. Upon creation the opposing team has 5 minutes to join the lobby. Once the 5 minutes have passed or the team has fully arrived, both teams are allowed to select their pre-submitted loadouts.

When passing the 10 minutes lobby creation time, the killer team is given a Standard Penalty I.

When passing the 5 minutes join time for the opposing team, the opposing team is given a Standard Penalty I.

When passing the 5 minutes loadout time the remaining players’ team, that are not yet readied up, are given a Standard Penalty II.

§9 Scoring, winning definition, forfeit right, game over

For scoring we use what Dead by Daylight provides us with - hooks, deaths and generators completed. Collectively referred to as Result-Scoring.

For survivors the achievable goal is to complete as many generators as possible and escape the game. For killers the achievable goal is to hook and kill as many survivors as possible, while preventing the survivors from completing generators to win the game.

Killers can score by hooking survivors or forcing hook stages and deaths. Survivors can score by completing generators and creating a harder win condition for the opponent by escaping a game or dying correctly.

The score is defined by the game result.

Primary-Score. How many generators are left, how many survivors have escaped and died.

Secondary-Score. How many first hook stages did the killer get, how many hook stages did he get in total.

When the primary-score is not enough - a tie; both teams have same escapes and kills on the same number of generators remaining - the secondary-score will define who won by splitting deaths into hook stages - 3 per survivor - and counting them together. If this is not enough and still results in a tie, then every first hook stage is added individually to the score. Whoever has the most hook stages and has at least one more first hook stage - when a survivor gets hooked for the first time - wins.

At least one more hook stage, first hook stage, death or generator uncompleted/completed than the opponent: clear result / win.

If there is a tie, despite the secondary-score: The game will be replayed or a tie breaker set must be played.

If the tie breaker set is also a tie, the original tied match will be repeated indefinitely until there is a clear result.

To prevent, in accordance with §9 number 8, indefinite matches, a team can agree to forfeit a match. Only one team has to agree upon a forfeit, yet the majority of that team’s players must agree to it.

To win a ‘game’ the majority of sets within the game must be won. Sets are defined by the type of best of. The number of the best of (e.g. best of 3) defines the number of sets (best of 3 = 3 sets and each one singular set played by a team = 6 singular set(s)).

When all survivors are downed (including individuals who are hooked and the perks Deliverance or Wicked are not in play or can be used or survivors are dead) and the perk Unbreakable is not in play or can be used, then the game is considered a ‘game over’ with the full hooks on all survivors and the current remaining generators being counted.

§10 Forfeits, unplayable conditions, disconnects

Teams have a general right to forfeit a match, set or game. By forfeiting a match, set or game they agree that they have lost the match, set or game and that the opposing team is awarded a win in the best of.

When forfeiting a match the forfeiting team is given a Standard Penalty I. for the upcoming match and when there is no upcoming match the team is given a Standard Penalty I. for the previously played match, which immediately changes the previous results.

When forfeiting a set the forfeiting team is given a Standard Penalty II. for the upcoming set (their killer game) and when there is no upcoming set they receive a Standard Penalty II. for the previous set, which immediately changes the previous results.

When forfeiting a game the forfeiting team is given a Suspension Penalty II.

Forced forfeits are forfeits that are enforced by the rules. Between forced forfeits and forfeits made by a team are no differences, they’re collectively referred to as forfeits.

Teams may forfeit without any penalty when they’re faced with unwinnable conditions. These conditions or special rights are explained by our staff ahead of time or when a match, set or game has ended and the conditions are, at our sole discretion, unwinnable or too hard to win.

Teams must report personal issues and personal unplayable conditions immediately. Upon reasonable error or unplayable conditions have been received at a reasonable time the team is granted permission to replay their game with the opponent at a later date without any penalties. Previously, before the report, already played matches were marked as valid and counted.

Game-related issues must also be reported immediately so that our team takes these into consideration and takes reasonable actions.

Teams and players are generally not allowed to disconnect a match or use the abandon feature, unless the staff is allowing it.

When players are disconnected by a game error or crash they must provide our team with a screenshot or recording of said error or crash to receive a Standard Penalty IV. and be able to restart the match.

When said screenshot is not provided or faked then the team is punished with a Standard Penalty III.

Game-related issues that lead to a disadvantage of one or more players lead to an immediate restart of the match without any additional penalties.

§11 Additional gameplay rules for survivor players

All survivors must be unique. A legendary skin of a survivor that changes the name and look completely does not count as a unique survivor.

When breaking this rule, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty I. for each repeated survivor and a Standard Penalty IV.

Survivors are not allowed to open chests. A chest is opened when the action progression bar is completed.

When breaking this rule, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty I. for each opened chest or when the opened chest is considered a mandatory change in the match result the violating team is given a Standard Penalty I. and a Standard Penalty IV.

Survivors are not allowed to attempt a self-unhook unless the perk Deliverance or Wicked is in place and can be used.

When successfully unhooking oneself, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty II. and a Standard Penalty IV.

When the attempt to unhook oneself is not successful, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty I.

Survivors may attempt to self-unhook when the game is over in accordance with §9 number 11 to speed up the tournament process. When a survivor successfully unhooks oneself they must remain unmoved for the killer player to down and hook them again.

Once all 5 required generators are completed, the survivor players are required to try and open the exit gates within 11 minutes.

When the survivors are hiding or not actively attempting to open the exit gates, they are punished with a Standard Penalty V.

The perk Saboteur may only be used to identify scourge hooks.

When using the perk Saboteur to sabotage hooks, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty V. including a Standard Penalty I.

§12 Additional gameplay rules for killer players

The killer may not bodyblock survivors for more than 10 seconds.

This rule does not apply to locations where the survivor is able to leave through more than one exit or when the survivor is able to use an item or perk to prevent the bodyblocking.

When breaking this rule, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty III.

When the hatch has been opened it must be closed immediately upon hearing or seeing it, but latest after 6 minutes of its opening.

When breaking this rule, the team is given a Standard Penalty V.

§13 During a game, voice chat & streaming

While a game is ongoing, no outer communication besides speaking to the own team in a voice chat is allowed.

When breaking this rule, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty V.

Penalties are enforced in real-time and irreversible.

Teams (each) may call for a break between sets once during a best of 3, best of 5, best of 7 or best of 9. Each break is a maximum of 6 minutes. Breaks can not be used consecutively. One break can be used per one set played.

During the game, team players must be in their own voice channel on the Discord server of the DBD Comp Network. They may not leave the channel unless dismissed by a member of staff.

Breaking this rule will result in the violating team receiving a Standard Penalty V.

When streaming the live event, a general delay of 25 minutes must be set. Also, a display of the DBD Comp Network must be seen at all times.

Breaking this rule will result in the violating player receiving a Suspension Penalty I and the team will receive an additional Standard Penalty I.

§14 Loadouts and restrictions

Loadout(s) is(are) defined as the entire and complete in-game equipment of a player. This includes characters, perks, items, add-ons and offerings.

All loadouts of each player must be created in compliance with our rules and balancing restrictions.

Breaking this rule will lead to a Standard Penalty I. - V. for the violating team based solely at the staff’s discretion. This includes all and every balancing offence.

Loadouts of a match-up have to be pre-submitted until a set deadline. But at least 7-days before the match starts.

Breaking this rule will give the violating team a collective Standard Penalty I. and an Official Warning.

Items and offerings are enforced and must be brought. The order in which the players equip them is not enforced and may be chosen at the team’s discretion. When teams have the chance to choose between two or more choices, they make such decisions at their sole discretion.

Violation is punished in compliance with §14 number 2.

Perks and add-ons can be brought and equipped, in compliance with the balancing restrictions, yet at the sole discretion of the player. Offences are explained in §14 number 2.

Violation is punished in compliance with §14 number 2

§15 Team roster, player appearance

Teams may consist of maximum 5 main players and minimum 4 (the “Main Roster”) and maximum 3 replacing players (the “Sub Roster”) leading up to a maximum team size of 8 players. Collectively referred to as Team Roster.

The team roster is registered by default with its registration data copied from the DBD Comp Network. Once registered for a tournament, the team roster is unchangeable. Players must change their team before registering for an event, if they wish to make changes.

The sub roster may replace a maximum of two main roster players during a tournament, but never the team captain, at any point.

In-game names must be kept clean. The player’s known by name must be shown first and may not be altered. Players must be recognizable and names must belong to the actual player behind the character.

Teams must use the original version of Dead by Daylight.

§16 Prizes, payouts

Prizes are given in pools with individual places. We only pay 1st, 2nd and 3rd place, unless stated otherwise.

Prizes can vary based on the event.

Any form of penalty automatically deducts the final payment for the team by 1% but at least 50€ for one penalty.

Each penalty a team has received—whether it was for a single player or the whole team—deducts the payment by 1% stacking together. The final percentage amount is deducted from the base prize.

Suspended players—at the time of payment—are counted as disqualified and receive treatment in accordance with §16 number 4.

Teams or players which have been disqualified during a tournament or after a tournament will not receive any form of payment.

The DBD Comp Network may not disqualify a team or player later than the payout time in §16 number 5. Teams disqualified after the payout time has passed receive the full amount of their prize. §16 number 3 still applies.

Prizes that are older than 4 months and not yet paid, will be kept by the DBD Comp Network.

The reason for no payment can be that the player is unable to receive a payment.

Prizes will be paid,—latest—60 days after the tournament grand final has been completed, to each player that has participated individually. To receive payment, a player must fill our mandatory T-D-01 (Tax & Self Declaration) form and submit it to payment@legal.dbdcompnet.com. The DBD Comp Network will only issue legal payments (e.g. no payments into restricted areas, no payments to players under the age of 18).

Prizes are not paid to someone else than the receiving player. So-called payment recipients to forward payments are not allowed.

§17 Rule enforcements

Every violation leads to an Official Warning unless punished otherwise within these rules.

The DBD Comp Network uses different levels of punishment called Penalty Levels. These levels are as follows:

Standard Penalty I. — Issues a -0.5 hook stage removal for the violating team and in the current match, unless instructed otherwise within a rule.

Standard Penalty II. — Issues a -1.5 hook stage removal for the violating team and in the current match, unless instructed otherwise within a rule.

Standard Penalty III. — Issues a -2.5 hook stage removal for the violating team and in the current match, unless instructed otherwise within a rule.

Standard Penalty IV. — Issues a match restart unless instructed otherwise within a rule.

Standard Penalty V. — Issues a forced forfeit of the violating team.

Official Warning — Issues a formal warning towards a team with no further punishment or penalty involved. Collecting 5 of these warnings issues a Suspension Penalty V. for the player or team.

Suspension Penalty I. — Issues a temporary suspension, only for the day it was issued. This penalty is lifted automatically when the clock (CET) hits 09:00 AM.

Suspension Penalty II. — Issues a temporary suspension for a week (7 days), starting when issued and ending after the 7th day including the original issuing time has passed.

Suspension Penalty III. — Issues a temporary suspension for a month (30 days), starting when it was issued and ending after the 30th day including the original issuing time has passed.

Suspension Penalty IV. — Issues a temporary suspension for six months (180 days), starting when it was issued and ending after the 180th day including the original issuing time has passed.

Suspension Penalty V. — Issues a permanent suspension, starting when it was issued.

When the Standard Penalty IV. is issued, the opposing team that has not violated the rules may change their entire loadouts in compliance with the balancing restrictions. The change must be completed within 10 minutes.

By breaking this rule, the violating team is given a Standard Penalty III.

When the violating team is found guilty of also having changed their loadouts, they are punished with a Standard Penalty V.

Despite §17 number 3 or similar rules, loadouts may never be changed.

Breaking this rule will result in the violating team receiving a Standard Penalty V.

When a team has received one penalty in each set, they are given a Standard Penalty V. for the entire game and the opposing team, when not in the same violation, receives the win.

A violation towards §4 is counted as a Standard Penalty II. unless instructed otherwise.

§18 Player and staff confidentiality, final say

Members of staff may not forward sensitive information from players to other players unless instructed to do so by the player they received the information from.

Members of staff are freed from §18 towards other members of staff for the DBD Comp Network.

Our staff has the final say in decisions. These decisions will and may not be discussed.

A repeated violation will result in a Suspension Penalty V. for the violating player or the entire team.
